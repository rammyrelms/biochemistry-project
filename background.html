Background and Literature Review

Medicinal plants have long been used in traditional medicine for the management of infectious diseases. Rosmarinus officinalis belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is known for its antimicrobial, antioxidant, and anti-inflammatory properties.

The emergence of antibiotic-resistant bacteria such as Staphylococcus aureus and Escherichia coli has increased the need for alternative antimicrobial agents derived from natural sources.

Objectives

General Objective

To determine the phytochemical composition, antimicrobial activity, and antioxidant potential of ethanolic leaf extract of Rosmarinus officinalis.

Specific Objectives

  • To conduct qualitative phytochemical screening of the extract
  • To evaluate antimicrobial activity against S. aureus and E. coli
  • To assess antioxidant activity using the DPPH assay

Hypotheses

  • H₀₁: No significant phytochemicals are present
  • H₀₂: No significant antimicrobial activity is observed
  • H₀₃: No significant antioxidant activity is detected
